## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are casual players between roughly 18 and 35 who enjoy cozy collection games, customization systems, and cyberpunk aesthetics.

They are likely to play in short sessions on desktop or mobile web, looking for a low-pressure but visually rewarding experience.

The main job to be done is:

- Build a beautiful futuristic fish tank
- Collect unusual fish species
- Unlock new decorations and combinations
- Return regularly for light maintenance, growth, and rewards

### Brand Personality

The intended personality is:

- Neon
- Wet
- Curated

The interface should feel:

- Atmospheric
- Slightly mysterious
- Rewarding to arrange and revisit

### Aesthetic Direction

This should not look like a cute cartoon aquarium or a sterile management dashboard.

The target visual direction is:

- Retro-futuristic bio-lab
- Dark mineral surfaces
- Glass reflections
- Industrial labels
- Electric cyan, acid green, and warning orange accents

The world should feel like a collectible display cabinet inside a neon research facility.

### Design Principles

- The tank itself is the hero of the interface.
- Decoration choices must affect both beauty and gameplay.
- The atmosphere should feel deliberate and premium, not noisy and random.
- Motion should emphasize floating, scanning, flickering, and life.
- The MVP should stay small enough to ship quickly, then expand through fish, decor, and progression depth.
